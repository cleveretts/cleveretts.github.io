Reflections on "The Ones Who Walk Away from Omelas"

Ursula K. Le Guin bounces around a direct idea with of Omelas, to make us wonder if being happy is worth it if you must make someone else suffer for it.

This challenges us to think of the cost of our joy at the expense of another's pain

The story has us consider the true value of happiness with a hidden cost.

This questions the foundation of happiness built on the foundation of suffering.

Le Guin's narrative forces us to consider the ethics of a happiness that depends on hidden cruelty
